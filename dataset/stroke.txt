Overview:
Stroke occurs when blood supply to the brain is interrupted or reduced.

Common Symptoms:
- Sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body
- Trouble speaking or understanding
- Vision problems
- Severe headache

General Advice / Self-care:
- Call emergency services immediately.
- Follow rehabilitation and physiotherapy post-stroke.
- Control risk factors like high blood pressure and diabetes.

Prevention Tips:
- Maintain healthy weight and diet.
- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol.
- Exercise regularly.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Any sudden stroke symptoms (FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call).

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
