Overview:
Malnutrition results from a diet lacking adequate nutrients, or from illness that prevents nutrient absorption.

Common Symptoms:
- Stunted growth in children
- Weakness and fatigue
- Weight loss or swelling
- Frequent infections

General Advice / Self-care:
- Eat balanced meals with protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats.
- Supplement vitamins and minerals if required.
- Ensure children have adequate nutrition.

Prevention Tips:
- Promote breastfeeding for infants.
- Access to affordable, nutritious food.
- Educate about healthy diets.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Severe weight loss or weakness.
- Children showing delayed growth or development.
- Persistent deficiencies despite diet improvement.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
