Overview:
Hand hygiene is the most effective way to prevent the spread of infections.

Common Symptoms of Poor Hygiene:
- Frequent respiratory or stomach infections
- Skin problems

General Advice / Self-care:
- Wash hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.
- Use sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol if soap is unavailable.
- Wash before eating, after using the toilet, and after coughing/sneezing.

Prevention Tips:
- Promote handwashing in schools and workplaces.
- Install hand hygiene stations in public areas.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Persistent infections despite good hygiene practices.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
