Overview:
Kidneys filter waste and balance fluids in the body. Poor kidney health can lead to chronic kidney disease.

Common Symptoms:
- Swelling in legs, ankles, or face
- Changes in urination
- Fatigue and weakness
- Persistent itching

General Advice / Self-care:
- Stay hydrated with adequate water intake.
- Limit salt and processed foods.
- Control blood pressure and diabetes.
- Avoid overuse of painkillers (NSAIDs).

Prevention Tips:
- Regular kidney function screening if at risk.
- Maintain a balanced diet with fruits and vegetables.
- Engage in regular physical activity.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Blood in urine or foamy urine.
- Persistent swelling or fatigue.
- Severe lower back pain.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
