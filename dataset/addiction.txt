Overview:
Addiction is a chronic disorder characterized by compulsive substance use or behaviors despite harmful consequences.

Common Symptoms:
- Cravings and loss of control
- Withdrawal symptoms when not using
- Neglecting responsibilities

General Advice / Self-care:
- Seek counseling or therapy support.
- Build healthy habits and routines.
- Join peer support groups (e.g., AA, NA).

Prevention Tips:
- Educate about risks of substance use early.
- Promote positive coping strategies for stress.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Inability to stop substance use despite harm.
- Severe withdrawal symptoms.
- Suicidal thoughts or behavior.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
