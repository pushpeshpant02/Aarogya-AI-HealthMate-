Overview:
Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection affecting mainly the lungs.

Common Symptoms:
- Persistent cough lasting >2 weeks
- Fever and night sweats
- Weight loss and fatigue
- Coughing up blood

General Advice / Self-care:
- Take antibiotics exactly as prescribed (6+ months).
- Cover mouth when coughing to prevent spread.
- Maintain good nutrition.

Prevention Tips:
- BCG vaccination (where recommended).
- Ventilate living spaces to reduce airborne spread.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Persistent cough with blood.
- Severe chest pain or breathing issues.
- Close contact with TB-positive individuals.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
