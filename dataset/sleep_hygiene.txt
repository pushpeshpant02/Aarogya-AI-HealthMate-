Overview:
Good sleep hygiene refers to healthy habits that improve sleep quality.

Common Symptoms of Poor Sleep Hygiene:
- Difficulty falling asleep
- Frequent awakenings
- Daytime fatigue and poor concentration

General Advice / Self-care:
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
- Create a calm, dark, and quiet environment.
- Avoid caffeine or heavy meals before bedtime.

Prevention Tips:
- Limit screen use before sleep.
- Exercise regularly but not late at night.
- Use the bed only for sleep and relaxation.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Persistent insomnia for weeks.
- Daytime sleepiness affecting daily life.
- Suspected sleep apnea (loud snoring, choking during sleep).

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
