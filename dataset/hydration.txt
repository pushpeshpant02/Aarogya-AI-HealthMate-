Overview:
Proper hydration is essential for body functions like temperature regulation, digestion, and circulation.

Common Symptoms of Dehydration:
- Dry mouth and thirst
- Fatigue and dizziness
- Dark-colored urine

General Advice / Self-care:
- Drink 2â€“3 liters of water daily (varies by age, activity, and climate).
- Eat water-rich foods like fruits and vegetables.
- Rehydrate with oral solutions after illness or heavy exercise.

Prevention Tips:
- Carry water during physical activity.
- Increase intake in hot weather or illness.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Severe dehydration signs: confusion, rapid heartbeat, no urination.
- Persistent vomiting or diarrhea.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
