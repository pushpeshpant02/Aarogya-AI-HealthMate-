Overview:
Cholera is a bacterial infection causing severe diarrhea and dehydration, often linked to unsafe water.

Common Symptoms:
- Profuse watery diarrhea
- Vomiting
- Muscle cramps
- Rapid dehydration

General Advice / Self-care:
- Drink oral rehydration solution (ORS).
- Rest in a cool environment.
- Continue eating light meals if possible.

Prevention Tips:
- Drink safe, clean water.
- Practice good sanitation and hygiene.
- Vaccination in high-risk areas.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Signs of dehydration (sunken eyes, low urine output).
- Persistent vomiting or diarrhea.
- Severe weakness or shock.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
