Overview:
Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral disease common in tropical regions, spread by Aedes mosquitoes.

Common Symptoms:
- Sudden high fever
- Severe headache and pain behind the eyes
- Muscle and joint pain
- Rash and mild bleeding

General Advice / Self-care:
- Rest and drink plenty of fluids.
- Use paracetamol for fever and pain.
- Avoid aspirin or ibuprofen due to bleeding risk.

Prevention Tips:
- Use mosquito nets and repellents.
- Eliminate standing water around homes.
- Wear protective clothing.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting.
- Bleeding gums or nosebleeds.
- Rapid breathing or dizziness.

Disclaimer: This is general advice, not a medical diagnosis.
Source: WHO / CDC / NHS
